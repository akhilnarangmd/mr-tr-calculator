MR Severity Cutoffs

Based on 2017 AHA/ACC & 2021 ESC guidelines for valvular heart disease. Grading integrates multiple parameters; no single value is definitive.

Mild (Grade I–II)
EROA: < 0.20 cm²
RVol: < 30 mL
RF: < 30%
Vena contracta: < 0.3 cm
Moderate (Grade III)
EROA: 0.20–0.39 cm²
RVol: 30–59 mL
RF: 30–49%
Vena contracta: 0.3–0.69 cm
Severe (Grade IV)
EROA: ≥ 0.40 cm²
RVol: ≥ 60 mL
RF: ≥ 50%
Vena contracta: ≥ 0.70 cm

Very Severe MR (guideline trigger for intervention)

Intervention Criteria (symptomatic)
EROA ≥ 0.40 cm² with symptoms (NYHA II–IV)
LV EF < 60% or LVESD > 40 mm
Additional Quantitative Markers
Pulmonary HTN at rest (RVSP > 50 mmHg)
New AF attributable to MR

PISA Geometry Correction

Standard PISA assumes 180° hemisphere. If the MR jet is constrained (eccentric), apply angle correction:
Corrected Flow = 2π × r² × Va × (α / 180°)
where α = opening angle of PISA surface in degrees

RVSP Estimation from TR (Bernoulli)

RVSP = 4 × (TR Vmax)² + RAP
RAP estimate: 3 mmHg (IVC <2.1cm + collapsible), 8 mmHg (indeterminate), 15 mmHg (IVC >2.1cm + non-collapsible)
PASP ≈ RVSP (in absence of RVOTO)
